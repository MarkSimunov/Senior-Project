Contest Rules


Materials
During the contest, students will be permitted to use writing utensils such as pencils and scratch paper. Standard calculators are permitted for use, but programmable calculators are not. The use of cellular phones and the internet is not allowed during the contest. Cell phone or internet use by any team member will results in an immediate disqualification of that person's entire team.

Computer
If you do not have a laptop, your team will be assigned to a pc during the contest. No computer storage media or devices will be allowed in the computer lab (Please contact a judge before the contest if you need to bring a storage device due to certain circumstances). All code must be entered using a computer.

Problems
When the contest begins, each team will be given seven programming problems to solve. Team members may work on these problems in any order or manner they choose. There will be exactly two and a half hours to work out solutions.

Testing
Each team may run their programs using their own test data as many times as they wish. Test programs very carefully prior to submission.

Judging of Programs
Judging of submitted programs will be done on the spot. Judges will run students code as the teams finish each problem. judges will test programs to determine whether if the program work correctly/incorrectly.

Standings
Teams will be ranked according to the number of problems they complete correctly. For example, a team that answered 7 problems correctly will finish ahead of a team that answered 6 probelms correctly. Tiebreakers will be settled based on accumulated time.

Prizes
Winners will be awarded after lunch.
